Step-by-Step Guide: How to Calculate Calories Needed Per Day

  1. Compute BMR: For men = 88.362 + (13.397 × kg) + (4.799 × cm) - (5.677 × age); women = 447.593 + (9.247 × kg) + (3.098 × cm) - (4.330 × age).
  2. Select activity multiplier: Sedentary 1.2, etc.
  3. Multiply BMR by multiplier for TDEE.
  4. Adjust for goals: -500 for loss, +500 for gain.
  5. Use our calorie calculator for instant verification.

What is Calorie Calculation? In-Depth Definition and History

Calorie calculation estimates energy expenditure to balance intake with needs. A calorie (kcal) is heat to raise 1kg water by 1°C. BMR is resting energy; TDEE includes activity.

The History of Calorie Calculation

Calories discovered in 1820s by Nicolas Clément. Harris-Benedict equation from 1919 (revised 1984) revolutionized it. Today, tools like our calorie burn calculator use it for modern nutrition.

Calorie Formula Breakdown

BMR via Harris-Benedict, TDEE = BMR × factor. Macros: Carbs 4kcal/g, proteins 4, fats 9.
