What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) represents the number of calories your body needs to perform basic life-sustaining functions such as breathing, circulation, and cell production while at rest. It accounts for approximately 60-70% of your total daily energy expenditure. Understanding BMR is crucial for effective weight management, as it forms the foundation for calculating Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE), which includes physical activity.

The Harris-Benedict formula, revised in 1984, is widely used for BMR estimation. For males: BMR = 88.362 + (13.397 × weight in kg) + (4.799 × height in cm) - (5.677 × age in years). For females: BMR = 447.593 + (9.247 × weight in kg) + (3.098 × height in cm) - (4.330 × age in years). This calculator applies these equations accurately.

Why Calculate BMR?

Knowing your BMR helps in creating calorie deficits for weight loss or surpluses for muscle gain. It's essential for athletes, dieters, and those monitoring metabolic health. Combine it with activity levels to get TDEE for a complete picture.

Comparison of BMR Formulas

Formula Pros Cons
Harris-Benedict Simple, widely validated May overestimate for obese individuals
Mifflin-St Jeor More accurate for modern populations Similar limitations

FAQ

  • How accurate is this BMR calculator? It uses established formulas but is an estimate. Consult a doctor for precise measurements via indirect calorimetry.
  • Can BMR change over time? Yes, with age, muscle mass, and hormonal shifts. Recalculate periodically.
  • What's the difference between BMR and TDEE? BMR is resting energy; TDEE includes activity. This tool calculates both.
