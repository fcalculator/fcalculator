How Does the Triangle Side Calculator Work?

This triangle side calculator uses mathematical laws like the Law of Sines (a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C)) and Law of Cosines (c² = a² + b² - 2ab*cos(C)) to compute missing sides and angles. For area, it applies Heron's formula (√[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)]) where s is the semi-perimeter. Medians are calculated as (1/2)√(2b² + 2c² - a²), inradius as area/s, and circumradius as (abc)/(4*area). The tool identifies triangle types (equilateral, isosceles, scalene, right, obtuse, acute) based on sides and angles.

Example: Enter Side A=5, Side B=5, Angle C=60° (degrees). Result: Equilateral triangle with Side C=5, angles 60° each, area ~10.825, visualized in charts showing equal distributions.

Triangle Properties and Formulas

Triangles are fundamental shapes in geometry. Use this calculator to explore properties like perimeter (a+b+c), heights (2*area/side), and more. Charts help visualize how changes in one side affect others.

  • Law of Sines: Ideal for angle-side calculations.
  • Law of Cosines: For side-side-angle cases.
  • Area Methods: Base*height/2 or (1/2)ab*sin(C).
