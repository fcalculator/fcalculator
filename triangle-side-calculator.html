How Does the Triangle Side Calculator Work?

This free online triangle calculator uses advanced formulas like the Law of Sines (a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C)) and Law of Cosines (c² = a² + b² - 2ab*cos(C)) to compute missing sides and angles. Area is calculated via Heron's formula (√[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)], where s = (a+b+c)/2) or base-height/2 methods. Medians, inradius (area/s), and circumradius ((abc)/(4*area)) are derived accurately. The tool classifies triangles as equilateral (all sides equal), isosceles (two sides equal), scalene (no sides equal), right (one 90° angle), obtuse (one angle >90°), or acute (all angles <90°).

Triangle Types and Properties

Triangles are three-sided polygons classified by sides or angles. Equilateral triangles have equal sides and 60° angles; isosceles have two equal sides; scalene have unequal sides. Right triangles follow Pythagorean theorem (a² + b² = c²); obtuse have one angle over 90°; acute under 90°. Our visualization draws tick marks on equal sides and concentric arcs on angles, similar to standard notations, but dynamically with your data.

SEO-focused: Searching for 'calculate triangle sides and angles online' or 'visualize triangle properties with formulas'? This tool provides comprehensive calculations and unique drawings for educational and practical use.

  • Pythagorean Theorem: For right triangles, hypotenuse c = √(a² + b²).
  • Law of Sines and Cosines: Solve any triangle type.
  • Area Formulas: Base × height / 2, (1/2)ab sin(C), or Heron's.
  • Medians and Radii: Intersect at centroid; inradius fits inside, circumradius around.
