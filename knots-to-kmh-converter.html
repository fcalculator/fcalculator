Understanding the Knot Scale

The knot represents speed in nautical miles per hour, vital for sea and air travel due to its alignment with Earth's latitude.

History of the Knot Scale

Developed in the 17th century via log lines, standardized in 1929 with the nautical mile, knots remain standard for navigation accuracy.

Understanding the Kilometers per Hour Scale

Kilometers per hour (km/h) is a metric speed unit for distance traveled in kilometers over one hour, prevalent in international transportation.

History of the Kilometers per Hour Scale

Introduced with the metric system in the 1790s, adopted globally via SI in 1960, km/h ensures consistency in engineering and regulations.

Speedometer in kilometers per hour.

The Conversion Formula Explained

The formula to convert knots to kilometers per hour is:

\[ \text{km/h} = \text{kn} \times 1.852 \]

Derived from the nautical mile equaling 1.852 kilometers.

Step-by-Step Calculation Example

For 10 knots: Multiply by 1.852 to get 18.52 km/h.
