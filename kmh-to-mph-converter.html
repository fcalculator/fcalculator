Understanding the Kilometers per Hour Scale

Kilometers per hour (km/h) is a metric unit of speed, expressing the distance in kilometers traveled in one hour, widely used in most countries for road signs, vehicle speedometers, and weather reports.

History of the Kilometers per Hour Scale

Introduced with the metric system in the late 18th century during the French Revolution, km/h became standard in Europe and globally through the adoption of the SI system in 1960, replacing older units like miles per hour in many regions for consistency in scientific and everyday measurements.

Understanding the Miles per Hour Scale

Miles per hour (mph) is an imperial unit of speed, indicating the distance in miles traveled in one hour, commonly used in the United States, United Kingdom, and some other countries for transportation and aviation.

History of the Miles per Hour Scale

Originating from the Roman mile and adopted in the British Imperial system in 1824, mph was standardized in 1959 under the international yard agreement, remaining prevalent in non-metric countries for speed limits and vehicle instrumentation.

The Conversion Formula Explained

The formula to convert kilometers per hour to miles per hour is:

\[ \text{mph} = \text{km/h} \times 0.621371 \]

Where mph is miles per hour and km/h is kilometers per hour. This derives from the exact relationship between the kilometer (1000 meters) and the mile (1609.344 meters).

Step-by-Step Calculation Example

For 100 km/h: Multiply by 0.621371 to get 62.1371 mph, typically rounded to 62.14 mph for practical applications.
