Understanding Random Number Generation

Random number generators (RNGs) are essential in various fields, from statistical sampling to cryptography and gaming. Our wheel-based RNG uses JavaScript's Math.random() function combined with customizable parameters to ensure transparency and fairness. The wheel visually represents the possible numbers, spinning to select one at random.

Key Features of Our Random Number Generator

  • Customizable Range: Set minimum and maximum values to define the pool of possible numbers.
  • Interval Control: Generate numbers at specific steps, like multiples of 5 or even numbers only.
  • Exclusion List: Remove specific numbers from consideration, ideal for avoiding duplicates or unwanted values.
  • Two Modes: Normal for unlimited picks, Elimination for progressive removal.
  • Visual Wheel: Engaging spinning animation for an interactive experience.
  • History Tracking: View past results with timestamps in New York time zone.

How the Wheel Works

The wheel is divided into segments based on your input range and interval. Each segment represents a possible number. When you spin, the wheel rotates randomly and stops on a segment, displaying the selected number in green at the center.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum range I can set?

The tool supports ranges up to 1000 numbers for performance reasons. For larger sets, consider breaking into multiple spins.

How does elimination mode work?

In elimination mode, once a number is picked, it's removed from the wheel for subsequent spins, ensuring no repeats until reset.

Can I save my results?

Yes, use the Save as PDF button to export your history table with timestamps.

Is the wheel truly random?

It uses JavaScript's pseudo-random generator, suitable for most uses but not for high-stakes applications requiring true randomness.
