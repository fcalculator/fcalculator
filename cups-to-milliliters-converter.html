Understanding the Cup Scale

The cup is a unit of volume commonly used in cooking and baking, primarily in the United States and other countries following imperial measurements, defined as approximately 236.588 milliliters for the US customary cup.

History of the Cup Scale

The cup originated from traditional English units and was standardized in the 19th century for culinary purposes. In the US, it was defined in 1896 as half a US pint, while metric cups (250 ml) are used in countries like Australia and Canada. Variations exist, such as the Japanese cup (200 ml), but the US cup remains prevalent in recipes worldwide.

Understanding the Milliliter Scale

The milliliter is a metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter, widely used globally for precise measurements in cooking, science, and medicine.

History of the Milliliter Scale
