Understanding the Jutro Scale

The jutro is a traditional unit of area used for land measurement, particularly in cadastral contexts in Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Serbia, where the katastarsko jutro equals 5,754.64 square meters, representing the area that could be ploughed in one morning.

History of the Jutro Scale

Originating from the German Morgen, the jutro was introduced during the Austro-Hungarian Empire and standardized for cadastral purposes in the 19th century. It varied regionally but was fixed at 5,754.64 m² in katastarsko jutro for administrative consistency in land registration and taxation, remaining in use for rural property assessments today.

Understanding the Square Meter Scale

The square meter is the SI unit of area, defined as the area of a square with sides of one meter, used worldwide for precise measurements in construction, real estate, and science.

History of the Square Meter Scale

Established in 1795 as part of the metric system during the French Revolution, the square meter was based on the meter defined by Earth's circumference. It was redefined in 1889 with platinum standards and in 1983 based on the speed of light, ensuring global uniformity in measurements.
