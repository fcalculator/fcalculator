Step-by-Step Guide: How to Calculate BMI Manually

  1. Convert height to meters (e.g., 175cm = 1.75m).
  2. Square the height (1.75 × 1.75 = 3.0625).
  3. Divide weight by squared height (70 / 3.0625 = 22.86).
  4. Compare to BMI categories: Underweight (<18.5), Normal (18.5-24.9), Overweight (25-29.9), Obese (>30).

What is Body Mass Index (BMI)? In-Depth Definition and History

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value derived from weight and height, used to classify underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obesity in adults. It's a screening tool, not a diagnostic one, but widely adopted by health organizations like WHO. For those asking "what is BMI," it's essentially a ratio that estimates body fat without direct measurement.

The History of BMI

BMI was developed by Belgian mathematician Adolphe Quetelet in the 1830s as the Quetelet Index for population studies. It was renamed Body Mass Index in 1972 by Ancel Keys to assess obesity risks. Today, it's a global standard, with adaptations like BMI for children using percentiles and BMI for Asians with adjusted categories due to different body compositions.

BMI Formula Breakdown

The core BMI formula is BMI = kg / m². For imperial units: BMI = (lbs × 703) / inches². This simplicity makes it accessible, but remember it's not perfect for athletes or elderly due to muscle mass variations.

BMI Chart Variations

BMI charts vary by age, gender, and ethnicity. For example, Asian adults may use lower cutoffs (overweight at 23+). Always consult a doctor for personalized advice.
