Understanding Sample Size Calculation

Formula for Sample Size

The sample size for an infinite population is calculated using the formula:

\[ n = \frac{Z^2 \cdot p \cdot (1 - p)}{E^2} \]

Where:

  • \( n \): Sample size
  • \( Z \): Z-score corresponding to the confidence level (e.g., 1.96 for 95%)
  • \( p \): Population proportion (e.g., 0.5 for 50%)
  • \( E \): Margin of error (e.g., 0.05 for 5%)

For finite populations, the adjusted sample size is:

\[ n_{\text{adjusted}} = \frac{n}{1 + \frac{n - 1}{N}} \]

Where \( N \) is the population size.

Why Sample Size Matters

A correctly calculated sample size ensures that your study results are statistically significant and representative of the population. Too small a sample may lead to unreliable results, while an overly large sample can waste resources.

Example Calculation

Suppose you want a 95% confidence level, a 5% margin of error, and a 50% population proportion for an infinite population:

  • Z-score for 95% confidence = 1.96
  • Population proportion (\( p \)) = 0.5
  • Margin of error (\( E \)) = 0.05

Plugging into the formula:

\[ n = \frac{1.96^2 \cdot 0.5 \cdot (1 - 0.5)}{0.05^2} = \frac{3.8416 \cdot 0.25}{0.0025} = 384.16 \]

Thus, a sample size of approximately 385 is required.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a confidence level?
The confidence level represents the probability that the sample accurately reflects the population, typically set at 90%, 95%, or 99%.

How do I choose a margin of error?
A smaller margin of error (e.g., 3%) provides more precise results but requires a larger sample size. A common choice is 5%.

What if I don’t know the population proportion?
Use 50% (0.5) as a default, as it maximizes the sample size and ensures conservative estimates.

When should I input the population size?
Include the population size for finite populations (e.g., a specific school or city). For large or unknown populations, leave it blank.
