Understanding the Knot Scale

The knot is a unit of speed used in maritime and aviation contexts, equal to one nautical mile per hour, approximately 1.151 miles per hour or 1.852 kilometers per hour.

History of the Knot Scale

Originating from sailing practices in the 17th century, the knot was measured using a "chip log" with knots tied at regular intervals on a line. It was standardized with the international nautical mile in 1929, becoming essential for navigation due to its relation to latitude degrees.

Understanding the Miles per Hour Scale

Miles per hour (mph) is an imperial unit of speed, indicating the distance in miles traveled in one hour, widely used in the United States and United Kingdom for road and rail transportation.

History of the Miles per Hour Scale

Derived from the Roman mile, mph was formalized in the British Imperial system in 1824 and standardized internationally in 1959, remaining key in non-metric countries for speed limits and vehicle performance.

Speedometer indicating speed in miles per hour.

The Conversion Formula Explained

The formula to convert knots to miles per hour is:

\[ \text{mph} = \text{kn} \times 1.15078 \]

Where mph is miles per hour and kn is knots. This derives from the definition of the nautical mile (1.15078 statute miles).

Step-by-Step Calculation Example

For 10 knots: Multiply by 1.15078 to get 11.5078 mph, typically rounded to 11.51 mph for practical applications.

Comparison of Speed Scales
