Understanding Rental Property Investments

Rental properties can be a lucrative investment avenue, offering both steady income through rent and potential appreciation in property value over time. This calculator helps you model various scenarios to assess viability.

The key formula for monthly mortgage payment is the standard loan amortization formula:

\[ M = P \frac{r(1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n - 1} \]

Where \( M \) is the monthly payment, \( P \) is the loan principal, \( r \) is the monthly interest rate, and \( n \) is the number of payments.

Cash Flow Analysis

Cash flow is calculated as monthly rent minus expenses like mortgage, taxes, insurance, maintenance, and adjusted for vacancy. Positive cash flow indicates profitability.

Return on Investment (ROI)

ROI measures the efficiency of your investment: \( ROI = \frac{Annual\ Net\ Income}{Initial\ Investment} \times 100 \). This tool projects ROI over the loan term.

Factor Impact on Investment
Down Payment Higher down payment reduces loan amount and interest costs but ties up more capital initially.
Vacancy Rate Accounts for periods without tenants; a conservative estimate (5-10%) is recommended.
Maintenance Costs Typically 1-2% of property value annually, but adjusted here as % of rent.
