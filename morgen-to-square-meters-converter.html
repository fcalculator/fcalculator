Understanding the Morgen Scale

The morgen is a traditional unit of area historically used in Europe and South Africa for land measurement, with the standard German morgen equaling 2,500 square meters, representing the area ploughable in one morning by a man with an ox team.

History of the Morgen Scale

Originating from medieval Germanic farming practices, the morgen varied regionally (e.g., 2,500 m² in Prussia, 2,767 m² in the Netherlands) but was standardized in some contexts during the 19th century. It influenced units like the South African morgen (0.8567 ha) and remains referenced in historical land records and cadastral surveys.

Understanding the Square Meter Scale

The square meter is the SI unit of area, defined as the area of a square with sides of one meter, used worldwide for precise measurements in construction, real estate, and science.

History of the Square Meter Scale

Established in 1795 as part of the metric system during the French Revolution, the square meter was based on the meter defined by Earth's circumference. It was redefined in 1889 with platinum standards and in 1983 based on the speed of light, ensuring global uniformity in measurements.
