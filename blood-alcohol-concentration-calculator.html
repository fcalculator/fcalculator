Understanding Blood Alcohol Concentration

Widmark Formula for BAC Estimation

The Widmark formula calculates BAC based on alcohol consumed, body weight, and distribution ratio.

\[BAC = \frac{A \times 5.14}{W \times r} - 0.015 \times H\]

Where \(A\) is alcohol in ounces, \(W\) is weight in pounds, \(r\) is gender constant (0.73 for males, 0.66 for females), \(H\) is hours elapsed.

Factors Influencing BAC

Gender affects distribution due to body composition; age impacts metabolism. Drink types vary in alcohol content, and time allows for elimination at ~0.015% per hour.

FAQ

What factors affect BAC accuracy? Metabolism varies by food intake, medications, and health; this tool provides estimates only.

Can I add custom drinks? Select types with standard ABV; for customs, adjust quantities accordingly.

Why include age? Older adults metabolize slower, slightly adjusting elimination rates.

What are legal BAC limits? Vary by country; e.g., 0.05% in many EU nations, 0.08% in US.

How does the chart work? It plots BAC from consumption start to full elimination.
