How the 401(k) Calculator Works

A 401(k) plan is a powerful retirement savings vehicle offered by employers in the United States, allowing employees to contribute pre-tax earnings, which grow tax-deferred until withdrawal. This calculator projects your future balance by simulating annual contributions, employer matches, salary growth, and investment returns over the years until retirement.

To use the tool, input your current age, planned retirement age, existing 401(k) balance, annual salary, contribution percentage, employer match details, expected salary increase, and investment return rate. The calculation assumes contributions are made annually, with returns compounded yearly. Employer matches are applied up to the specified limit.

Key Formulas Used

The growth is calculated iteratively each year:

  • Adjust salary: \( \text{New Salary} = \text{Previous Salary} \times (1 + \frac{\text{Salary Increase \%}}{100}) \)
  • Your contribution: \( \text{Contribution} = \text{Salary} \times \frac{\text{Contribution \%}}{100} \)
  • Employer match: \( \min(\text{Contribution} \times \frac{\text{Match \%}}{100}, \text{Salary} \times \frac{\text{Match Limit \%}}{100}) \)
  • Total addition: Contribution + Match
  • Earnings: \( (\text{Balance} + \text{Total Addition}) \times \frac{\text{Return \%}}{100} \)
  • New balance: Previous Balance + Total Addition + Earnings

For overall future value approximation without yearly adjustments, a simplified annuity formula can be used: \[ FV = P \times \frac{(1 + r)^n - 1}{r} + \text{Initial} \times (1 + r)^n \] where \( P \) is annual total contribution, \( r \) is return rate, \( n \) is years to retirement. However, this tool uses detailed yearly simulation for accuracy.

Assumptions and Considerations

The projection assumes consistent annual contributions and returns, without accounting for taxes on withdrawals, fees, or market volatility. Actual results may vary based on economic conditions. Contribution limits for 2025 are $23,500 for under 50, plus catch-up for older individuals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 401(k) plan? A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored retirement savings plan where employees can contribute a portion of their salary on a pre-tax basis, often with employer matching contributions, allowing for tax-deferred growth.

How does employer matching work? Employers may match a percentage of your contributions up to a certain limit of your salary, effectively providing additional funds to your retirement savings.

What rate of return should I use? Historical stock market returns average around 7-10% annually after inflation, but adjust based on your risk tolerance and investment mix.

Are there contribution limits? Yes, for 2025, the employee contribution limit is $23,500, with an additional $7,500 catch-up for those 50 and older. Total contributions including employer match are capped at $70,000.

When can I withdraw from my 401(k)? Withdrawals before age 59½ may incur penalties, except in specific cases. Required minimum distributions start at age 73.
