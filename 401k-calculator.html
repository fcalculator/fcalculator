Understanding 401(k) Plans

A 401(k) is a powerful employer-sponsored retirement savings plan in the United States, offering tax-deferred growth on contributions and earnings. Named after the relevant section of the Internal Revenue Code, it allows employees to contribute pre-tax dollars, reducing taxable income while building wealth for retirement. Many employers match a portion of contributions, effectively providing "free money" to boost your savings.

The growth of your 401(k) can be modeled using compound interest formulas. For example, the future value \( FV \) of your balance with annual contributions can be expressed as:

\[ FV = P \times (1 + r)^n + C \times \frac{(1 + r)^n - 1}{r} \]

Where \( P \) is the initial principal, \( r \) is the annual return rate, \( n \) is the number of years, and \( C \) is the annual contribution (including matches).

Adjusting for salary increases and inflation adds complexity, but our tool handles it seamlessly.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the 2025 contribution limit? $23,500 for under 50, plus catch-up for older workers.
  • Should I choose Roth or Traditional 401(k)? Roth for tax-free withdrawals if you expect higher taxes in retirement. Compare with our Roth IRA tool.
  • How does vesting work? Employer matches may vest over 3-6 years; check your plan.
