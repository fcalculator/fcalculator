How the 401(k) Calculator Works

This tool projects 401(k) savings by simulating annual contributions, employer matches, salary increases, and compound returns. Start with your current balance, then loop through years to retirement: adjust salary for growth, calculate contributions (employee % of salary, capped employer match), add to balance, apply return rate. Distributions estimate annual withdrawals over retirement years, adjusted for inflation. The line chart visualizes balance growth, and the table details yearly breakdowns. Assumptions include constant rates, no withdrawals/taxes until retirement, and U.S.-based limits (e.g., 2025: $23,500 deferral).

Mathematical Formulas and Derivations

The core projection uses compound interest with annual payments, adjusted for increasing contributions:

\[ FV = PV (1 + r)^n + PMT \frac{(1 + r)^n - 1}{r} (1 + g)^y \]

Where \( FV \) is future value, \( PV \) current balance, \( r \) return rate, \( n \) years, \( PMT \) initial contribution, \( g \) salary growth, \( y \) year. In practice, iterated annually for precision. Employer match: \( match = \min(emp\_contrib, salary \times limit\%) \times match\% \). Distributions: \( dist = FV / (life - ret\_age) / (1 + inf)^{ret\_years} \).

Factors Influencing 401(k) Growth and Retirement Savings

Contribution and Match Dynamics

Higher employee contributions maximize tax-deferred growth; employer matches (e.g., 50% up to 6%) amplify savings but are capped. Salary increases compound contributions over time.

Return and Inflation Impacts

Expected returns (historical avg. 6-8%) drive growth, but inflation erodes purchasing power; conservative estimates (e.g., 7% return, 2% inflation) balance optimism.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 401(k) plan? A U.S. employer-sponsored retirement account with tax-deferred contributions and potential employer matches.

How does employer matching work? Employers add funds (e.g., 50% of your 6% contrib), capped; always contribute enough to max it.

Can I withdraw early? Yes, but with 10% penalty + taxes before 59½, except hardships; loans possible without penalty if repaid.

What if returns vary? Tool assumes fixed; rerun with averages (historical 6-8%) for scenarios.

Why include inflation? Adjusts distributions for real purchasing power; higher inflation reduces effective withdrawals.
