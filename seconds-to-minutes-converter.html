Understanding the Second Scale

The second is the base unit of time in the International System of Units (SI), defined by the frequency of radiation from the cesium-133 atom, used for precise measurements in science, computing, and everyday timing.

History of the Second Scale

Originally defined as 1/86,400 of a mean solar day in the 19th century, the second was redefined in 1967 based on the cesium atom's hyperfine transition for atomic precision, enabling advancements in GPS, telecommunications, and physics.

Cesium atomic clock used for defining the second.

Understanding the Minute Scale

The minute is a unit of time equal to 60 seconds or 1/60 of an hour, commonly used for timing in sports, cooking, and transportation.

History of the Minute Scale

Derived from Babylonian astronomy and the sexagesimal system, the minute was formalized in medieval Europe with mechanical clocks. It was integrated into the metric system in 1793 and remains essential in modern timekeeping.

Analog clock showing one minute.

The Conversion Formula Explained

The formula to convert seconds to minutes is:

\[ \text{min} = \text{s} \div 60 \]

Where min is minutes and s is seconds. This derives from the definition of one minute equaling 60 seconds.

Step-by-Step Calculation Example

For 120 seconds: Divide by 60 to get 2 minutes.
